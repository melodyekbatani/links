R&B

visual archive

Each medium carries its own traditions and ways of practice. R&B, or Rhythm and Blues, originated in African American communities in the 1940s. It combines elements of jazz, gospel, and blues, and is characterized by its soulful vocals, strong rhythms, and often romantic themes and continues to be a significant and influential genre in the music industry today. In R&B these traditions emerge through sound, image, and feeling. This compilation traces defining moments, highlighting the artistry of the medium while exploring identity, intimacy, and image-making within its visual culture.

    Our Perspective

    • From

      Trenton Soto

      This collection explores my subjective relationship to contemporary R&B music through sound, image, and context. Bringing together album cover art, criticism, and artist references, this collection reflects on what it means to make R&B music today. It explores how visuals shape the listening experience, how genre labels influence how music is understood, and how artists balance intimacy, expression, and expectation.

    • From

      Melody Ekbatani

      My name is Melody, though I may be the least musical person you'll ever meet. Just ask my former piano, flute, violin, or guitar teachers (my parents really wanted it to work out).Through this project, I was introduced to Trent's admittedly awesome collection, which helped me understand why I felt disconnected in the first place. I began to wonder if I could not participate through performance, maybe there were other ways to pay attention. This website looks closely at the art, visual culture, and expression of R&B, a deeply human genre shaped by the stories and cultural moments it carries. So maybe this is the way I find myself through music. Anyways, I really enjoyed making this, I hope you like it too.
